Seoul

One of my favorite cities that I have explored recently is Seoul, the capital of South Korea. It was my first time traveling to Asia, which has been an ambition of mine since back in middle school. I started to learn a lot more about the culture of South Korea during 2020, when everyone had to quarantine due to COVID. I indulged myself in countless videos that took place in South Korea and found myself wanting to learn more about their cuisine, history, and more. Seoul is a popular tourist attraction, partially because it's the epicenter of K-pop and K-dramas, which has been globally popularized. The city is a blend of modern and traditional elements that showcases the rich history and stunning landscapes, while also being at the forefront of fashion, cosmetics, and technology.

Cafes & Bakeries

Seoul has an abundance of aesthetic cafes and bakeries. I'm a big foodie, especially when it comes to sweets and drinks. I had a huge list of possible cafes and/or bakeries that my friend and I could visit in 9 days. The baked goods are top tier, offering innovative flavor combinations and made fresh with high-quality ingredients. They excel at pastries and incorporate their cultural influence with modern techniques.

Shops

Something that I love about the shops in Seoul is that they hold such a large variety of accessories, cosmetics, and cute knick knacks that is hard to come across here in Washinton. Koreans are well known to have some of the best skincare products with their top selling store, Olive Young so it's a must to swing by. The Daiso in Myeongdong is 12 stories, each floor is designated with it's own theme such as kitchen supplies or stationary. I highly recommend checking out all kinds of shops, some of them have jewelry that lines multiple of the walls and the prices are decent for the most part too! Something that I found especially unique is that there are many shops that take place underground since the city is very populated with buildings and people.

  • Olive Young
  • Daiso
  • Mimiline
  • Nyu Nyu

Tourist Attractions

Seoul has many kinds of tourist attractions that hold their history, innovation, and more about their culture. These are some places that I visited. One of the most popular attractions is Gyeongbokgung Palace, which is the largest grand palace in Seoul as it served as the main palace for the Joseon dynasty. Many tourists and locals visit wearing hanboks, which is traditional Korean clothing, to feel one with the culture and take pretty pictures. Rich history is shown and preserved through the architecture in Seoul, and it's incorporated into the modern streets that are plentiful with aesthetic shops and tasty restaurants.

  • Dongdaemun Design Plaza
  • Ikseon-dong Hanok Street
  • Myeongdong Street
  • Gyeongbokgung Palace

Night Markets

The night markets are a staple to go to if you're going Seoul. Many buildings light up the night life and food vendors bring out their stalls to sell a variety of unique and traditional Korean street snacks.
